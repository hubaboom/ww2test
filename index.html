Causes of World War II

Treaty of Versailles
  • Signed in 1919 after WW1
  • Germany forced to accept war guilt
  • Germany lost land and colonies
  • Military limited to 100,000 soldiers
  • Germany had to pay massive reparations
  • Created resentment and economic instability
Rise of Dictators
  • Germany – Adolf Hitler (Nazi Party)
  • Italy – Benito Mussolini (Fascist regime)
  • Japan – Militarist government and Emperor worship
  • Dictators promoted nationalism and expansion
Expansionism
  • Germany remilitarised the Rhineland (1936)
  • Germany annexed Austria (1938)
  • Germany demanded Sudetenland from Czechoslovakia
  • Japan invaded Manchuria (1931)
  • Italy invaded Ethiopia (1935)
Appeasement
  • Britain and France tried to avoid war
  • Munich Agreement allowed Hitler to take Sudetenland
  • Believed satisfying demands would stop further aggression
  • Instead it encouraged Hitler to expand further

Key Timeline of WWII

  • 1939 – Germany invades Poland
  • 1940 – Germany conquers Denmark, Norway, Belgium, Netherlands, France
  • 1940 – Battle of Britain
  • 1941 – Germany invades Soviet Union
  • 1941 – Japan attacks Pearl Harbor
  • 1942 – Battle of Midway
  • 1942–43 – Battle of Stalingrad
  • 1944 – D-Day Allied invasion of France
  • 1945 – Germany surrenders
  • 1945 – Atomic bombs dropped on Japan

Major Battles

Battle of Britain (1940)
  • Germany attempted to defeat Britain with air attacks
  • Luftwaffe bombed cities and airfields
  • Royal Air Force defended the country
  • First major defeat for Hitler
Battle of Stalingrad (1942–1943)
  • Germany attempted to capture Soviet industrial city
  • Urban warfare caused massive casualties
  • Soviets surrounded German army
  • Turning point on Eastern Front
D-Day (1944)
  • Allied invasion of Normandy, France
  • Largest amphibious invasion in history
  • Marked beginning of liberation of Western Europe

The Holocaust

  • Nazi policy to exterminate Jews and other groups
  • Approximately 6 million Jews killed
  • Victims sent to concentration camps and death camps
  • Major camps included Auschwitz, Treblinka, and Sobibor
  • Other victims included Roma, disabled people, political prisoners and LGBTQ people

Australia in WWII

  • Australia joined the war in 1939 after Britain declared war
  • Australian troops fought in North Africa and Europe
  • Important campaign: Kokoda Track in Papua New Guinea
  • Japan bombed Darwin in 1942
  • War increased industrial production and changed society

End of World War II

  • 1945 – Allied forces invaded Germany
  • Adolf Hitler committed suicide
  • Germany surrendered May 1945 (VE Day)
  • USA dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki
  • Japan surrendered September 1945 (VJ Day)

Consequences

  • 70–85 million deaths worldwide
  • Europe devastated economically
  • United Nations formed
  • Cold War began between USA and USSR
