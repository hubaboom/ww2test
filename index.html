Causes of World War II

Treaty of Versailles
  • Signed in 1919 ending WW1
  • Germany forced to accept full responsibility for the war
  • Lost major territory including Alsace-Lorraine
  • Military limited to 100,000 soldiers
  • No tanks, submarines or air force allowed
  • Required to pay massive reparations
  • Led to anger, economic crisis, and desire for revenge
Rise of Dictatorships
  • Economic instability after the Great Depression allowed extremist leaders to rise
  • Germany – Adolf Hitler and the Nazi Party
  • Italy – Benito Mussolini and Fascism
  • Japan – militarist government controlling the emperor
  • These regimes believed in expansion and military power
Failure of the League of Nations
  • International organisation meant to maintain peace
  • Had no army to enforce decisions
  • Japan invaded Manchuria (1931)
  • Italy invaded Ethiopia (1935)
  • Germany rearmed and remilitarised Rhineland
  • The League failed to stop these actions
Appeasement
  • Britain and France tried to avoid another war
  • Allowed Germany to take Sudetenland in 1938
  • Believed Hitler would stop after limited expansion
  • Instead it encouraged further aggression

Detailed WWII Timeline

  • 1931 – Japan invades Manchuria
  • 1935 – Italy invades Ethiopia
  • 1938 – Germany annexes Austria (Anschluss)
  • 1938 – Munich Agreement gives Germany Sudetenland
  • 1 Sept 1939 – Germany invades Poland
  • 3 Sept 1939 – Britain and France declare war
  • 1940 – Germany conquers Denmark, Norway, Belgium and France
  • 1940 – Dunkirk evacuation
  • 1940 – Battle of Britain
  • 1941 – Germany launches Operation Barbarossa (invades USSR)
  • 7 Dec 1941 – Japan attacks Pearl Harbor
  • 1942 – Battle of Midway shifts Pacific war
  • 1942–43 – Battle of Stalingrad turning point
  • 1943 – Allies invade Italy
  • 6 June 1944 – D-Day invasion of Normandy
  • 1944 – Battle of the Bulge
  • April 1945 – Soviet forces capture Berlin
  • May 1945 – Germany surrenders (VE Day)
  • Aug 1945 – Atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki
  • Sept 1945 – Japan surrenders (VJ Day)

Major War Fronts

European Western Front
  • Germany used Blitzkrieg tactics to rapidly invade Western Europe
  • France fell in 1940
  • Britain remained unconquered after Battle of Britain
  • Allies returned to Europe during D-Day invasion in 1944
  • Western Allies pushed toward Germany from France
Eastern Front
  • Germany invaded the Soviet Union in 1941
  • Largest and deadliest front of the war
  • Major battles:
    • Stalingrad
    • Kursk
    • Leningrad siege
  • Soviets eventually pushed Germany back to Berlin
Pacific Front
  • War between Japan and Allied powers
  • Japan rapidly conquered Southeast Asia
  • US adopted island hopping strategy
  • Major battles:
    • Midway
    • Guadalcanal
    • Iwo Jima
    • Okinawa
North African Front
  • Fought between Axis and Allied forces
  • Germany sent Afrika Korps led by Erwin Rommel
  • Major battle: El Alamein
  • Allies eventually pushed Axis forces out of Africa

The Holocaust

  • Nazi genocide targeting Jews and other minorities
  • Approximately 6 million Jews murdered
  • Victims sent to concentration camps and extermination camps
  • Major camps included Auschwitz, Treblinka, and Sobibor
  • Other victims included Roma people, disabled people, political prisoners and LGBTQ individuals

End of the War & Consequences

  • Germany collapsed after Allied and Soviet invasion
  • Hitler committed suicide April 1945
  • Germany surrendered May 1945
  • Atomic bombs dropped August 1945
  • Japan surrendered September 1945
  • Estimated 70–85 million deaths worldwide
  • United Nations formed
  • Cold War began between USA and USSR
