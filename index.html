Causes of World War II

World War II began due to a combination of political, economic and ideological factors.

  • Treaty of Versailles (1919) punished Germany severely after WW1.
  • Economic instability during the Great Depression caused widespread poverty.
  • Rise of dictators including Hitler, Mussolini and militarist leaders in Japan.
  • Expansionism where countries tried to gain land and resources.
  • Appeasement allowed Hitler to take territory without resistance.
  • Failure of the League of Nations to enforce peace.

Major War Fronts

Western Front

Germany invaded Western Europe using blitzkrieg tactics. France fell in 1940 but Britain resisted. The Allies later returned to Europe with the D-Day invasion in 1944.

Eastern Front

The Eastern Front between Germany and the Soviet Union was the largest and deadliest front of the war. Major battles included Stalingrad, Kursk and the Siege of Leningrad.

Pacific Front

The Pacific War involved Japan fighting against the United States and Allied powers. Japan initially conquered many territories but was eventually pushed back through island hopping campaigns.

North African Front

Axis forces led by General Erwin Rommel fought Allied forces in desert warfare across North Africa. The turning point was the Battle of El Alamein.

The Holocaust

The Holocaust was the systematic genocide carried out by Nazi Germany. Approximately six million Jews were murdered, along with millions of other victims including Roma people, disabled individuals and political prisoners.

Victims were sent to concentration camps and extermination camps such as Auschwitz and Treblinka.

Consequences of WWII

  • Over 70 million deaths worldwide
  • Creation of the United Nations
  • Division of Germany into East and West
  • Start of the Cold War
  • Major technological and political changes
